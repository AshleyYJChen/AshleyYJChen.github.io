Hoopers

Find basketball matches quickly, track your stats, improve your ball game.




Overview

Hoopers is a web based application created by a team of three for a class project in UC San Diego. I experienced the flow of user interaction design and utilized programming skills for routing and front-end engineering. The app Hoopers tackles the issue of finding pick-up basketball games and also allows players to track their game performance. It is a combination of needfinding and a dash of fun that led us to this app idea, as we initially bonded over chatting about basketball.

Challenge

Casual players shouldn’t need to go through multiple means to join in games or go through a hassle to remember their stats every game.




How might we create an app to help users have a better balling experience?







User Research

Needfinding

In the beginning, our team talked to fellow students and people we saw on the courts to learn about their point of view. We asked our target audience, recreational basketball players, what is appealing about basketball and how they go about finding basketball games. We also interviewed other people to gauge their level of interest in basketball and to possibly see what is holding them back from playing more.




Analysis

Insights we found were

  • There is difficulty of finding basketball games for some people, especially those who don’t have a group of friends to ball with.

  • 4/5 mentioned the inconvenience of showing up to a court hoping to play in a team but there are too few people.

User Testing

We initially tested our users on the two paper prototypes we created by having them walk through the prototypes as we present them. We received heuristics evaluations and made changes to create a final iteration of the paper prototypes our group did.

Some feedback we noted:

  1. Create an account page for sign in because the app contains a profile.

  2. Navigation bars to have clearer label names for consistency with the page functions.

  3. Navigation bars include 5 instead of three for better organization of functionalities and visibility of the system status. (My games, stats, friends, my profile, find games).

  4. List games joined in a separate tab for efficiency of use, since the app revolves around playing basketball.

  5. Stats to display information through a chart instead of individual drop-downs because it improves the aesthetic of the page design and conveys information better.

  6. Automatic navigation after creating or joining a game for flexibility and efficiency of use.

  7. More options for flexibility in teams and game creating to make adding games more freeform.

  8. Add an accessible button to add stats and make games in all pages for better visibility of the system.

User Testing Continued

Additional user testing was done after the web app was created. We captured the breakdowns and successes the users had while using our app to further improve it’s design and intuitiveness. Based on the struggles found in some usability of our app, like creating games and logging stats, we decided on changes to be made.

  • Create an obvious way to add games, the current check box is too small. (img.2)

  • Navigation tabs to have clearer label names for consistency with the page functions.

  • Inputting forms need better signifiers for functions like drop down menu. (img.3)

  • Include 5 navigation tabs instead of three for better organization of functionalities and visibility of the system status. (My games, stats, friends, my profile, find games).





Final Product

The final iteration of the completed, coded app contains the vital pages to locate games, create games, add and track self statistics, see favorited games, and a profile page.
